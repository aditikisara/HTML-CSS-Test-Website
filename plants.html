PLANTS



CURRY LEAVES

Curry leaves are used in many Indian dishes and has a very aromatic scent. It is commonly added in the dish at the beginning of cooking to maximize the overall flavor. It is also used in some forms of medicine.

They produce small white flowers and can grow into a small tree. These plants need partial shade to properly grow. When they are ready, you can pluck off a stem and use it in any culinary dish.

EGGPLANT

Eggplants are really common in Mediterranean cuisines. It is large egg-shaped berry and is commonly purple. It is closely related to tomatoes and even potatoes. Raw eggplants taste a bit bitter, but it becomes much more tender when cooked. Often times, it is a meat substitute in vegetarian dishes.

There are many ways to cook eggplants. From baked to garnish, it is truly a versatile ingredient.

TOMATOES

Tomatoes are one of the most common plants people grow in their gardens. It is a wonderful base for many soups and stews. When they get closer to harvesting time, they can get really heavy for their branches. As a result, many people stake their tomato plants to keep them upright.

They are normally grown in warmer climates and will die annually in colder weather. The plant is covered with tiny hairs which will act as roots if it ever touches the ground.

LYCHEES

These sweet fruits are mainly grown in Southeast Asia and India. It’s a fruit that has a red, bumpy outer skin and a white, translucent, sweet inside with a single seed. They don’t need much attention as long as they have a lot of moisture around the roots. The trees grow within a couple of years.

It’s important to eat only the lychees that have ripened (ripe ones have a red outer shell). Eating them before ripening can be dangerous.
